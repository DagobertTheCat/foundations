What makes memorable, meaningful, and transformative learning happen?

In this part of the activity, you'll turn your experiences into a list of characteristics of memorable, meaningful, and transformative learning.
{% include icon-team.html %}With your team:

Part 1

Think about the stories shared in the previous activity. Work with your team to brainstorm a list of the characteristics of learning you noticed in these stories. It might help you to think about how you would complete the following sentences:

Learning is most memorable when: ______________.
Learning is most meaningful when: ______________.
Learning is most transformative when: ______________.

Tips

  • Try completing the phrases from multiple perspectives, including teachers, learners, and the learning enviornment, as well as motivational and contextual factors.

Part 2

Next, take a look at the following list of ideas shared by previous participants in this mini-course (use the up and down arrows to scroll through the responses).

Talk together with your team about the following questions:

  • What themes or categories are you noticing?
  • How can we balance seemingly contradictory ideas? For example, how do we make learning environments both "challenging" and "comfortable"?
  • What are the most important ideas for you as a learner? For the learners you work with?

Something missing? You can contribute your ideas to this list!

{% include icon-notebook.html %}In your notebook:

Your Top-5 List

Based on your discussions, create your own personal "Top-5" list of the characteristics of memorable, meaningful, and transformative learning that are most relevant specifically for you. Write this list down in your notebook. We will come back to this list later!
